The experience starts with a splash screen. A glowing lightbulb icon pulses at the center, then gives way to the brand name in a warm gold typeset. A line draws across the screen, and the tagline fades in — all setting the tone before the page even loads.

As the full page reveals, you land on a hero section with a dark, sophisticated backdrop. A subtle gold grid stretches across the background, and it responds to mouse movement — creating an interactive glow that follows your cursor. The headline anchors the brand message, while a 3D lightbulb sits to the right. The bulb has a letterform embedded in the glass, and you can click and drag to rotate it. It glows brighter on hover — a small interactive moment that makes the page feel alive. Three clear calls to action sit beneath the headline: book a consultation, view services, or request a quote.

Scrolling down, the Why Watts section uses animated counters that tick up as they enter view — showing metrics like projects delivered and years of experience. Below the numbers, six cards arranged in a two-by-three grid explain core value propositions. Each card has a consistent structure: an icon, a title, and a short description. The layout is clean and strictly aligned.

The page continues into the Featured Work section. Four project cards sit in a two-by-two grid. Each card has a thumbnail image, a category tag, a title, and a stack of technology tags at the bottom. Hovering over a card reveals an overlay with a prompt to view the project. Clicking into one opens a modal with extended details and an image gallery.

Below that is a section for tools and reports. Two main tools are featured side by side, along with a row of three smaller planning tools beneath them. Each card has a title, a short description, and a call-to-action button with a gold arrow.

Further down, the Services section presents six offerings in a three-by-two grid against a dark background. Each card includes an icon, a title emphasized in gold, and a short body description. The layout is consistent, creating a predictable reading rhythm.

Scrolling again brings you to a five-step process laid out horizontally. Each step has a numbered badge and a short explanation, connected by a line to show progression from discovery through launch and support. The horizontal layout makes the full journey visible at a glance.

A testimonial carousel follows, featuring quotes from past clients. It auto-advances every few seconds, and users can navigate manually using arrow buttons or dot indicators. On smaller screens, longer quotes are truncated with a prompt to see more.

Finally, the contact section combines a call-to-action card with a simple three-field form beneath it. Name, email, and message — no unnecessary fields. The modal versions for consultation bookings and quote requests are more detailed, adding project type, budget, and timeline fields tailored to each purpose.

The footer closes the page with a five-column layout: the brand mark, navigation links, services, technologies used, and contact information. A back-to-top button appears as you scroll down, letting users jump back up in one click.

Overall, the site uses a controlled dark-and-gold palette, consistent card-based layouts, and subtle interactive moments — scroll-triggered counters, a mouse-following grid glow, and a rotatable 3D element — to guide the user through the content without overwhelming them.
